TO RUSH

Long ago a professor in a summer course of mine on world religions drew my attention to something.
This something runs through my mind
like legs on a treadmill.
He called to attention how much of the human experience is a rushing one.
We rush to wake up.
We rush to gobble down our breakfast.
We rush to throw on flimsy clothes that look "the part."
We rush to pack up our gizmos and gadgets.
We rush to check once, twice,
three times total sometimes
that we have our phone,
our wallets
and our keys.
We rush down the busy streets to get to our destination.
We rush through our long and grueling days.
We rush through our fantasies of what our dream lives would be
and what they would look like.
When its time to go we rush to pack up once more.
Continuing to rush home,
Rush through dinner or our glasses of wine
or quite frankly whatever our unwinding activities may be.
We rush to bed.
Ready to rush all over again once more the next day.
